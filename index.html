Editorial Essay

Only Dead Fish Go With the Flow

By JUNKTEE · 6 min read

There's a stretch of coastline outside Jeddah where the tide leaves things behind it doesn't mean to. Bottle caps. A shoe. Once, memorably, a fax machine. We started collecting photos of it before we started a clothing brand, which tells you something about the order things actually happened in.

"The fish doesn't choose to go with the flow. It's already dead. That's the whole joke — and it's not really a joke."

Everyone in this industry wants to tell you they're sustainable. Fewer want to tell you what that actually costs, or admit when the number doesn't move the way they hoped. We'd rather be the second kind of brand. It's less comfortable. It's also the only version of this we're interested in building.

So: a recycling mark, worn loud instead of printed small on a bottle nobody reads. A shirt that's honest about being hemp because hemp actually uses less water, not because "sustainable" sounds nice on a hangtag. And a dead fish, because sometimes the truest thing you can say, you have to say sideways.
